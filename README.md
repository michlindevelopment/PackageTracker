## 🤔 Why AliTrack?

Ever ordered something from AliExpress and then... forgotten about it for two months? 😅

You open the app, scroll for ages trying to find that one earphone case you ordered, copy a tracking number, paste it into a sketchy tracking website that's 80% ads, and find out it's been sitting at customs for a week. **There has to be a better way.**

There is. It's this app. 👇

## ✨ What it does

### 📲 One-tap import
Open AliExpress in the app, tap **Import**, and *boom* — every order you've placed is in your list. Photos, names, tracking numbers, the lot. No copy-pasting, no typing tracking numbers wrong.

### 🚚 Real tracking, not guesses
AliTrack talks directly to **Cainiao** — the actual logistics company that moves your AliExpress packages around the world. So instead of "we have no info on this number," you get every scan from "left warehouse in Shenzhen" to "out for delivery near you."

### 🔔 Notifications that actually matter
Get pinged only when something *real* happens — when your package ships, when it clears customs, when it's out for delivery. Not "your package is being processed" 47 times in a row.

### 🗂️ Three simple lists
- **In Transit** — stuff that's on its way 🛫
- **Received** — stuff that arrived ✅
- **Not Yet Sent** — stuff you ordered but the seller hasn't shipped yet 🕒

### 📸 Add a photo
Snap a picture of what you ordered (or use the AliExpress thumbnail) so you can actually remember which package is which. No more "wait, what was this one again?"

### 🌗 Looks good day or night
Material 3 design with **light, dark, and follow-the-system** themes. On newer phones it even picks up your wallpaper colors. 🎨

### 📅 At-a-glance everything
Estimated delivery, days in transit, a clean timeline of every scan event, where it came from, where it's going. All on one screen.

## 🚀 How to use it

### Adding a package the easy way
1. Tap the **+** button
2. Tap **Import from AliExpress**
3. Sign into AliExpress (only once — the app remembers)
4. Tap the orders you want to track
5. That's it. Seriously. ✨

### Adding a package the manual way
1. Tap the **+** button
2. Type or paste your tracking number
3. Give it a name (optional — but helpful when you have 12 things in transit 😅)
4. Snap a photo (also optional, also helpful)
5. Hit **Save** ✅

### Marking something as received
Got your package? 🎁
- **Long-press** the card → **Mark as Received**
- Or tap the package and use the menu

(Honestly, AliTrack usually figures it out on its own when Cainiao reports it as delivered.)

## ❓ FAQ

<details>
<summary><b>Is it free?</b></summary>
<br>
Yep. 100%. No ads, no premium tier, no email signup. 🆓
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Will it work for non-AliExpress packages?</b></summary>
<br>
AliTrack uses Cainiao's tracking network, which mostly handles AliExpress, Temu, and similar Chinese cross-border shipments. If your package number is from Cainiao, it'll work. Local couriers (DHL, UPS, FedEx, your country's postal service) aren't supported — sorry! 😬
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Why isn't it on the Play Store?</b></summary>
<br>
For now it's distributed directly through GitHub Releases — that keeps it simple and free of Play Store policies. Maybe one day! 🤷
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Does it work in the background?</b></summary>
<br>
Yes! AliTrack quietly checks for updates on your packages a few times a day and pings you when something changes. No need to keep the app open. 😴
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>What about my privacy?</b></summary>
<br>
Everything stays on your phone. AliTrack only talks to two places: <b>Cainiao</b> (to ask "where's my package?") and <b>AliExpress</b> (when you tap import). No analytics, no accounts, no cloud, no ads. 🔒
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>How do I update the app?</b></summary>
<br>
Pop back to the <a href="https://github.com/michlindevelopment/PackageTracker/releases/latest">Releases page</a> and grab the newest APK. Install it on top of the old one — your packages and photos stay put. 🔄
</details>
